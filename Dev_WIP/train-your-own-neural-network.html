Train Your Own Neural Network

This is a hands-on project where you can train your own Neural Network starting from a blank state, to get a brief insight into the steps involved. In the previous project on opacity, we looked at classification. Here we will look at an example of regression. Regression is where the output is on a scale or a dial.

You can control the movement of a circle with your body. You will go through the following main steps: (1) Collecting training data, (2) training the model, and (3) prediction or making an inference. The slider can be used to move the circle from left to right. Our aim is to replace the slider and control the movement using our own body. For this project, the movement is retricted from the left to the right. In the steps below, I mention moving your hand left to right, but you can use any other part of your body such as legs, head movement, etc. You can also use another object in your hand, as long as it is a progressive motion.


Steps to Train The Below Neural Network

Step 1

Move slider to the left end. Place your hand over the yellow spot.

Step 2

Click “Add example image” multiple times.

Step 3

Move slider bit to the center. Move your hand to the center of the screen.

Step 4

Click “Add example image” a few times.

Step 5

Repeat until you reach the right end of the slider.

Step 6

Click “Add example image” a few times.

Step 7

Click “Train” once.

Step 8

The model will be trained in a few seconds and you can control the ball with your hand movements.


The ball is waiting to be moved based on your hand movements. Train the Neural Network to control the movement of the ball.


You trained it using 3 main steps






  1. Collecting training data: Training data is a data set representative of the data the machine learning model is provided with to solve the problem it’s designed to solve. The training data needs to be properly prepared—randomized, de-duped, and checked for imbalances or biases that could impact the training.
  2. Training the model: Choosing an algorithm and training the it to create the model. Training is an iterative process with adjusting multiple settings until the algorithm returns the correct result most of the time.
  3. Prediction or making an inference: Using the model with new data and, in the best case, for it to improve in accuracy and effectiveness over time. Where the new data comes from will depend on the problem being solved.
